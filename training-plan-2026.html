The shape of the year

The one friction point in your layout: heavy legs Tuesday → intervals Wednesday. Your legs won't be 100% fresh for the reps. That's an acceptable trade most of the year, but three rules keep it from costing you:
  • Tuesday box squats stay explosive and sub-maximal — power off the box, leave 1-2 reps in the tank. The goal is stiffness and pop, not a quad-trashing.
  • Run quality in the AM, lift in the PM where your schedule allows (6+ hrs apart) so neither session eats the other.
  • During the Speed Block only, dial Tuesday lower back ~10-15% so Wednesday's VO2 reps stay genuinely fast — speed is the whole point of that phase.

Why the downhill obsession: mountain 50ks aren't won on the climbs, they're lost on the descents when your quads give out. Your partial-depth box squats and the eccentric/heel-drop work in the gym are exactly the durability insurance that lets you hammer down late in the race when the field is hobbling. Run the descents in training so race day isn't the first time.

Strength — the three sessions

Built off the Liam Henderson structure that had you bulletproof: one upper (chest-led), one lower (durability + heavy partial squats + calf isos), one plyo (power + chest + core). Same template all year, intensity periodised to match the running phase. Chest gets volume every session because — your words — you're not training to be scrawny.

Fuelling & recovery — the non-negotiables

  • Carbs around quality. Eat properly before Mon tempo, Wed intervals and Sat long. You can't run fast or far underfuelled, and you can't grow a chest in a deficit either.
  • Protein for the chest goal. Aim ~1.6-2.0 g/kg bodyweight daily. Without it the lifting is wasted and you'll lean out, not fill out.
  • Sleep is the cheat code. 7-9 hrs. It's where the speed block and the strength actually land. KOTH and event weeks wreck this — plan running light around them.
  • In-race fuel, rehearsed. 60-90 g carbs/hr for the 50ks, dialled in training not on race day. The Last Legend means eating every loop — practice it.
  • Listen over the schedule. A niggle caught early costs a session; ignored it costs a race. Especially the next 11 days off a layoff.
